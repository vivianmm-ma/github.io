The Vagus Nerve: Your Body's Superhighway to Calm

An exploration of how stress impacts our physical well-being and how we can reclaim our body's natural state of balance.

The "Rest & Digest" System

The vagus nerve is the main component of the parasympathetic nervous system, our body's primary calming mechanism. It acts as a brake on our stress response, helping us relax, recover, and regulate emotions. A healthy vagus nerve has a strong "vagal tone."

A Brain-Body Superhighway

As the longest cranial nerve, it wanders from the brain down through the neck and chest to the abdomen, connecting to and influencing nearly every major organ system along the way.

The Lifelong Impact of Childhood Stress

Adverse Childhood Experiences (ACEs) can program the nervous system for a lifetime of hypervigilance, with profound consequences for adult health.

ACEs & Autoimmune Disease Risk

Research shows a strong correlation between the number of ACEs and the likelihood of developing an autoimmune condition in adulthood. High cumulative stress dysregulates the immune system.
