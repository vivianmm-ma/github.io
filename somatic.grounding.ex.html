Three-Part Standing Meditation

A Practice for Somatic Balance and Nervous System Regulation

This sequence is an invitation to cultivate interoceptive awareness—the ability to sense the internal state of your body. By moving through three distinct postures, we explore the balance of complementary energies (Yin and Yang): the stability of grounding, the integration of our core, and the expansiveness of reaching. This structured practice provides a safe container for your creative, moment-to-moment experience.

Preparation

Find a comfortable place to stand with your feet about shoulder-width apart. Allow your knees to be soft, not locked. Let your spine be long and your shoulders relaxed. Take a few gentle breaths to settle into your body and the present moment.

Posture I: Grounding (Yin Aspect)

The Posture (1 Minute): Allow your arms to hang comfortably by your sides. Let your elbows have a slight bend. Feel the pull of gravity on your body, connecting you to the floor and the earth beneath it. This posture emphasizes stability, reception, and physical presence.

Somatic Inquiry

  • Notice the sensations in the soles of your feet. Do you feel pressure, texture, or temperature?
  • Can you sense the weight of your body settling downwards through your skeleton?
  • Where do you feel the most stability in this posture? Your feet, your legs, your hips?
  • What is the felt sense of "groundedness" in your body right now?

Posture II: Centering (Core Integration)

The Posture (1 Minute): Slowly bring your arms forward to about chest height. Bend your elbows and face your palms toward each other, as if holding a large, lightweight ball. This posture brings awareness to your body's center, integrating the upper and lower halves.

Somatic Inquiry

  • What subtle sensations (warmth, tingling, coolness) do you notice in your hands and arms?
  • Bring your awareness to your breath or heartbeat. Notice its natural rhythm without needing to change it.
  • What is the quality of the space you are holding? Does it feel expansive, dense, or neutral?
  • How does this posture affect your sense of being centered in your own physical space?

Posture III: Reaching (Yang Aspect)

The Posture (1 Minute): Gently raise your arms overhead, continuing the upward movement by rising onto the balls of your feet. Lengthen through your spine toward the ceiling. This posture emphasizes expansion, gentle activation, and creating space in the body.

Somatic Inquiry

  • Notice the feeling of length through your spine and the top of your head. Where do you feel expansion?
  • As you balance, notice the micro-adjustments in your feet and ankles. What is this experience of active stability like?
  • What is the quality of your awareness in this expansive state? Is it focused, broad, or something else?
  • In this posture, what is your relationship to the physical space around and above you?

Integration

Slowly lower your heels, then let your arms float back down to your sides. Stand in stillness for a few moments, noticing any shifts in your physical or internal state. The goal is not to feel a certain way, but simply to notice what is present. Carry this awareness with you as you move into the rest of your day.
