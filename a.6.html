Build Your Sensory Toolkit

The First Step in Regulation

Before we can tackle complex trauma responses, we need to establish a baseline of safety. A sensory toolkit is a practical, powerful first step that anyone can begin, regardless of your starting point. It's about consciously identifying what your nervous system finds calming (soothers) and what it finds agitating (triggers).

Not everyone knows what's bothering them or has the words to describe it. This worksheet is designed to help you discover your unique sensory profile. Remember, sensory accommodations are a standard of care for neurodivergent individuals, but they are beneficial for everyone. This is universal self-care.

From the Author's Toolkit

As a clinician who has spent the last eight years refining these techniques, I rely heavily on my own sensory toolkit. The goal is portability and discretion. For example, I always have a small, smooth fidget toy in my pocket. No one knows it's there, but it provides constant, calming tactile input. I have bins of different fidgets at home and in my office because my ADHD brain craves novelty.

I also use scent heavily. A small bottle of calming lotion at my desk or a soothing spray in my bag allows for a quick sensory reset. Taste is powerful, too. These small, consistent acts of self-regulation are the foundation of a resilient nervous system.

How To Use This Worksheet
