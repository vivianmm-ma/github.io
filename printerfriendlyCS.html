Congruence: A Harmony of Being

Psychologist Carl Rogers spoke of Congruence as a state where our actions and experiences (Creation) are in harmony with our core self and values (Structure). It‚Äôs when the garden we cultivate reflects the home we've built. It feels genuine and true.

When out of balance, we feel Incongruence. This is the dissonance between who we are and what we do.

The World We Live In: Society's Balancing Act

This internal balance is deeply affected by the cultural structures around us.

How We Cope: Internalizers vs. Externalizers

Internalizer

Asks: "Is my house built wrong?"

Often emerges from rigid cultures where not fitting the mold is seen as a personal failure.

Externalizer

Asks: "Why is everyone else's house so bad?"

Thrives in cultures that reward selfishness, blame, and a lack of accountability.
