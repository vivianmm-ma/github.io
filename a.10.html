Therapeutic Implications

Healing involves shifting the nervous system out of these "stuck" survival states. This is achieved through bottom-up approaches that communicate safety directly to the body, bypassing the cognitive brain:

  • Gentle Respiratory Regulation: Techniques like the "Tonal Sigh" or humming stimulate the vagus nerve and require less muscular effort than deep breathing, which can be mechanically impossible due to diaphragmatic rigidity.
  • Targeted Somatic Release: Employing gentleness in areas of "armor." For instance, Constructive Rest Pose helps release the tense hip flexors of the Flight response, while small, intentional movements help "unwind" the tense fascia where physical trauma is held.
  • Titration and Dosage: The most crucial principle is to practice all exercises in small, 30-60 second increments and immediately returning focus to a neutral anchor if a feeling becomes overwhelming. This teaches the nervous system that you are in control.
