What is Neurodivergence?

It's a Different Operating System: Like Mac or PC.

Neurodiversity is the understanding that human brains naturally vary. These variations are valuable differences, not defects. This guide explores two of these powerful "operating systems."

The Current Challenge: A Mismatch

Both processing styles are essential. However, modern society often imposes neurotypical rules and demands on everyone, which can be profoundly disabling for neurodivergent individuals.

Unspoken Social Rules

The expectation of neurotypical communication (e.g., constant eye contact, indirect language, performing emotions) can lead to burnout, anxiety, and self-betrayal for neurodivergent individuals.

Rigid Societal Demands

The rigid 9-to-5 work schedule and environments saturated with overstimulating sensory information are not built for neurodivergent processing, leading to exhaustion and distress.

The Solution is Cultural Competence

We must move beyond a "deficit" model. The path forward requires building a shared language, dropping assumptions, and creating environments that accommodate and value all operating systems.

Understanding Common Neurotypes

Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD)

A neurodevelopmental difference characterized by distinct social communication styles, unique sensory processing, and often intense, focused interests. It is not an intellectual disability or a language disorder, though co-occurring differences can exist. Many autistic individuals have average or above-average intelligence.

Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder (ADHD)

A neurodevelopmental difference impacting attention regulation, impulsivity, and hyperactivity. It is not a deficit in attention itself, but a difference in how it is regulated. Individuals with ADHD often exhibit creativity, hyperfocus on engaging tasks, and rapid ideation. It is not an intellectual disability.
