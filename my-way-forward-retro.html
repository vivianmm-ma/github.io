Vivian Mitchell, MA Applied Clinical Psychology, APCC
*not medical advice*, consult your provider

Introduction: My Cage (Structure) and My Freedom (Creativity)

Most days, my body felt like a cage. Perhaps a prison, one I couldn’t escape from. At its height, I could hardly leave my house, or even my bed, for weeks or months, sometimes dragging myself out of bed on unimaginable amounts of medication to make it through a course. I couldn’t move many muscles of my body, it felt like my entire body was swollen, I woke up vomiting and heaving often, and I could hardly hold myself up to take a five-minute walk with my dog. I’d have many pre-fainting (pre-syncope) episodes, triggered by an overactive fight-or-flight response. Doctors were unable to find much besides hypermobile Ehlers Danlos Syndrome and a history of many broken bones. I had partial paralysis throughout my body, a pinched nerve in my spinal cord, suspected nerve compression, and chronic muscle tension that physicians audibly gasped at. My heart rate would often dip into the high 40s and low 50s, then spike to 120+ when standing more than a minute or two. My hips were entirely immobile, and I unknowingly walked with my lower back, moving my legs. I couldn’t move my neck much at all, and I compensated with other muscles. Diagnosed with chronic illness, I found myself in a medical system that left me with more questions than answers. I was constantly seeking and not finding, and I felt I had run out of time.

Now: Today, my body is no longer a prison. It feels more like a creative canvas, a space of freedom and expression. The partial paralysis has lifted, and my nervous system is no longer trapped in a constant state of hyper-vigilance. My body feels strong and safe. The journey from my past self to my present reality was not a straight line, but was built upon a deliberate framework of structure and creativity.

The Framework and Philosophical Basis

This framework is not a quick fix. It is a slow, methodical path that honors the body’s need to build a new foundation of trust. This is a deliberate process that harnesses the power of neuroplasticity—the brain's ability to rewire itself. By consciously and consistently creating new patterns, we can literally build new neural pathways that support peace and safety, rather than a state of chronic fight-or-flight. My philosophy is that we must first learn to build structure and create a safe and stable environment for ourselves before we can begin to safely let go and express our creativity. In short, the first step is to learn to trust the ground beneath us before we can learn to fly.

This is not a quick fix, and please work with your provider. I will not be diagnosing or giving medical advice. The information you are about to read is not intended as medical advice but as a philosophical approach to healing, a map for the territory you are about to navigate. This is a collaborative process with our bodies, not a fight against them.

Trauma Response Physical/Mental Manifestation
Fight (Structure) Tension, high heart rate, bracing, muscle guarding, anxiety, obsession with control, trying to “push through,” rigid thinking, clenched jaw.
Flight (Creativity) Restlessness, running away, trying to keep busy, a sense of disembodiment or floating, avoidance, scattered thinking, fidgeting.
Freeze (Structure) Feeling “stuck,” frozen, immobilized, partial paralysis, dissociation, feeling heavy, sluggish, brain fog, feeling like you’re in a trance.
Flow (Creativity) Feeling safe and secure enough to move freely without tension, grounded, centered, fully present, feeling light and joyful, creativity, spontaneity, and play.

Step 1—Building the Foundation

Our bodies are not the enemy; they are trying to protect us. The first step is to give our body what it needs to feel safe: a solid foundation. Our body needs to feel strong and safe before it will release its tension. This is a collaborative process between mind and body.

1.1 Reclaiming Your Breath: The First Foundation

Your breath is your anchor to the present moment. Most people with an overactive nervous system tend to breathe from their chest. Learning to breathe from your belly, also known as diaphragmatic breathing, is a powerful first step in calming the nervous system. By gently focusing on your breath, you can begin to create a new, safe pattern that signals to your brain that you are okay. This simple practice is the first step in creating a new, more resilient neurological feedback loop that can effectively regulate our internal state, moving us from a reactive to a responsive mode.

1.2 Somatic Education & Gentle Strength

By gently strengthening and moving our bodies, we are not just building physical muscle. We are sending new signals to our brain, teaching it that the body is no longer in a state of constant threat. This is a direct application of neuroplasticity, as we create new connections that link movement with safety and calm. Consider practices like gentle stretching, walking, tai chi, or body-weight exercises to reconnect with your body without triggering a threat response.

1.3 Create a Safe Healing Environment

This isn't just about a physical space. It's about creating a safe emotional and social environment. This may mean setting boundaries, finding a supportive community, or a trusted therapist. Healing is a collaborative process; we are not meant to do it alone.

Step 2—Releasing the Armor

Once you have a foundation of safety and strength, you can begin to release the rigid patterns of tension. Releasing the chronic tension held in our bodies is a physical act with a neurological consequence. As the "armor" softens, we are deactivating old, rigid neurological pathways that were formed during times of stress and trauma. In their place, we are now free to build new, more flexible pathways that support a state of 'flow' and creative expression.

Reflections

This framework represents my path forward, but it is not the end of the journey. The beauty of neuroplasticity is that it is a lifelong process. I am still learning, still growing, and still finding new layers of freedom and creativity within myself. The key is not to achieve a static state of 'healed,' but to embrace the ongoing process of building a life where we can continuously adapt, learn, and live in 'flow.'
