Scientific Foundations and Key References

The structure and items in this assessment are informed by several key scientific concepts. The primary sources below provide a deeper understanding of the principles at work.

  • Porges, S. W. (2011). The polyvagal theory: Neurophysiological foundations of emotions, attachment, communication, and self-regulation. W. W. Norton & Company.
  • Dana, D. (2018). The polyvagal theory in therapy: Engaging the rhythm of regulation. W. W. Norton & Company.
  • Ogden, P., & Minton, K. (2000). Sensorimotor psychotherapy: One method for processing traumatic memory. Traumatology, 6(3), 53-72.
  • Siegel, D. J. (1999). The developing mind: Toward a neurobiology of interpersonal experience. Guilford Press.
  • Levine, P. A. (1997). Waking the tiger: Healing trauma. North Atlantic Books.
  • Critchley, H. D., & Garfinkel, S. N. (2017). Interoception and emotion. Current Opinion in Psychology, 17, 1–7.
