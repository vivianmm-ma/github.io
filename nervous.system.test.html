System Manual: Understanding Your Nervous System

Vagal Tone

Think of the Vagus Nerve as the main quest line connecting your brain to your body, controlling your "rest and digest" and "social engagement" systems. Vagal Tone is like your resilience stat—the higher it is, the better you can recover from stress (debuffs) and feel safe and connected. It's the core of the mind-body connection.

Somatic

Simply means "relating to the body." A somatic approach is one that honors the body's wisdom, sensations, and stored memories as a primary path to healing, rather than focusing only on the thinking mind.

Sympathetic Nervous System

This is your "fight or flight" system—the gas pedal. It mobilizes you for action, providing the energy to face a challenge or escape danger. It's your body's "Combat Mode."

Parasympathetic Nervous System

This is your "rest and digest" system—the brake pedal. It helps you calm down, connect, and heal. It has two important pathways: Ventral Vagal (for safety and connection) and Dorsal Vagal (for shutdown).

Dorsal Vagal (Shutdown)

This is the oldest part of the brake pedal, an emergency brake that leads to shutdown, freeze, or collapse when a threat is too overwhelming to fight or flee. It’s your system’s “Low Power Mode.”

Dysregulation

This is what happens when your nervous system gets stuck in a state that doesn’t match your current reality. Your gas pedal (Sympathetic) or emergency brake (Dorsal Vagal) is stuck "on," even when you're in a safe environment.
