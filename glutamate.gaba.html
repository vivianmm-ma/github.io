Not All Carbs Are Created Equal

The type of carbohydrate consumed directly impacts the stability of the brain's fuel supply.

Simple Carbs (The Spike & Crash)

Refined sugars and white flour cause a rapid blood glucose spike, followed by a crash. This volatility can worsen anxiety, irritability, and brain fog.

Complex Carbs (Slow & Steady Fuel)

Found in oats, beans, and vegetables, their fiber content ensures a slow, sustained glucose release, providing stable energy for hours.

Evidence-Based Nutrients for Regulation

Certain supplements may help support the glutamate-GABA balance. Always consult a doctor before starting new supplements.

Magnesium

Helps reduce excess excitatory (glutamate) signaling by acting as a natural blocker for its receptors.

Vitamin B6 (P5P)

A critical "helper molecule" required for the enzyme that converts glutamate into calming GABA.

Zinc

Helps inhibit excitatory signals and also plays a role in the synthesis and release of GABA.

N-Acetylcysteine (NAC)

An antioxidant that helps regulate and restore the overall balance of glutamate in the brain.

L-Theanine

An amino acid (from green tea) that can block glutamate receptors and may increase GABA levels.
